Simple grammar

Simple grammar are an essential part of any language.

Pronouns

In the case of personal pronouns, it will perform the function of indicating the subject, it may be explicit or not. Depending on the context and the conjugated verb, it is possible to understand who the prayer is referring to: “Sou de São Paulo” (I am from São Paulo).

Portuguese Subject Pronoum Object Pronoum
Eu I Me
Tu You You
Ele He Him
Ela She Her
- It It
Nós We Us
Vós You You
Eles They Them


We also have possessive pronouns, which indicate who has possession

Adjetive Pronouns
My meu, minha, meus, minhas Mine meu, minha, meus, minhas
Your teu, tua, seu, sua Yours teu, tua, seu, sua
His dele His dele
Her dela Hers dela
Its teu, tua, seu, sua, teus, tuas, seus, suas (coisas) Its teu, tua, seu, sua, teus, tuas, seus, suas (coisas)
Our nosso, nossa, nossos, nossas Ours nosso, nossa, nossos, nossas
Your teus, tuas, seus, suas Yours teus, tuas, seus, suas
Their deles, delas Theirs deles, delas


Demonstrative pronouns indicate where an element is according to the subject.


To demonstrate proximity

Number [Número] Demonstrative Pronouns Pronomes demonstrativos

Plural These estes, estas

To demonstrate distance

Gender


Words have gender, even if they are objects. Normally words have masculine and feminine versions, masculine ones end with “o” while feminine ones with “a”. But of course there are exceptions

Masculine = Masculino
Feminine = Feminino

Obrigado (Masculine) | Thank you
Obrigada (Feminine) | Thank you

Menino (Masculine) | Boy
Menina (Feminine) | Girl
Médico (Masculine) | Doctor
Médica (Feminine) | Doctor

Aluno (Masculine) | Student
Aluna (Feminine) | Student
Professor (Masculine) | Teacher
Professora (Feminine) | Teacher

Exceptions
Criança (Used for both genders) | Child
