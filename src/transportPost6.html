Disembarked in Brazil: What to Do Next?

Getting around is easy with reliable public transport options, making your exploration of Brazil both seamless and enjoyable. Dive into our essential tips and resources to ensure a smooth and unforgettable start to your adventure.

Welcome to Brazil! Whether you're here for a brief visit or a longer stay, there are several key steps and tips to help you smoothly navigate your arrival and maximize your time in this vibrant and diverse country. From practical advice on handling entry formalities to insights on making the most of your Brazilian adventure, this guide will set you off on the right foot. Here's what you need to know to get started:


1. Clear Customs and Immigration

After landing, your first stop will be at customs and immigration. Be sure to have all necessary documents ready:
Passport: Ensure it's valid for at least six months from your date of entry.
Visa: Check if you need a visa for Brazil and have it ready if required.
Customs Declaration: Fill out the customs declaration form provided on the plane or at the airport.

2. Currency Exchange

Brazil's official currency is the Brazilian Real (BRL). You can exchange money at the airport, but for better rates, consider doing it at a bank or an authorized exchange office in the city. Most major cities also have ATMs that accept international cards.

3. Getting a SIM Card

Stay connected by purchasing a local SIM card. Major providers like Vivo, TIM, Claro, and Oi offer prepaid SIM cards with various data plans. You can buy these at the airport, mobile shops, or convenience stores. Make sure your phone is unlocked to use a Brazilian SIM.

4. Transportation from the Airport

Depending on your destination, you have several options for getting from the airport to your accommodation:
Taxis: Available at all major airports. Use licensed taxi services or apps like 99 or Uber for safety and convenience.
Airport Shuttles: Many airports offer shuttle services to central locations or major hotels.
Public Transit: Larger cities like São Paulo and Rio de Janeiro have bus and metro services connecting the airport to the city.

5. Navigating the City

Brazil's cities offer a variety of transportation options to help you get around:
Buses: An extensive network covers almost all areas. Platforms like ClickBus and Guichê Virtual make booking intercity buses easy.
Subway and Trains: Cities like São Paulo and Rio de Janeiro have efficient subway systems for quick travel across the city.
Ride-Hailing Apps: Services like Uber and 99 are widely used and convenient for getting around urban areas.

6. Accommodation

Ensure you have your accommodation booked in advance. Brazil offers a wide range of lodging options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the top hotels in Brazil:
Copacabana Palace (Rio de Janeiro): This iconic hotel offers luxurious accommodations with a stunning view of Copacabana Beach. It's known for its opulent rooms, excellent service, and historic charm. Fasano (São Paulo): Located in the upscale neighborhood of Jardins, Hotel Fasano is renowned for its sophisticated atmosphere, exceptional dining, and elegant rooms. Belmond Hotel das Cataratas (Iguazu Falls): Situated within the Iguazu National Park, this hotel provides exclusive access to the falls. Guests can enjoy luxurious amenities and breathtaking natural scenery. Emiliano (Rio de Janeiro and São Paulo): The Emiliano hotels are known for their modern design, personalized service, and gourmet dining options. Both locations offer a chic and comfortable stay. Tivoli Mofarrej (São Paulo): Positioned in the heart of São Paulo, this hotel features spacious rooms, a rooftop pool, and stunning city views, making it a favorite among business and leisure travelers.

7. Plan Your Itinerary

Brazil is vast and diverse, offering everything from bustling cities to pristine beaches and lush rainforests. Consider visiting:
Rio de Janeiro: Known for its iconic Christ the Redeemer statue, Sugarloaf Mountain, and Copacabana Beach.
São Paulo: Brazil’s financial hub, famous for its cultural institutions and vibrant nightlife.
Amazon Rainforest: For a unique ecological adventure.
Iguazu Falls: One of the largest and most spectacular waterfall systems in the world.

With these steps and tips, you're well on your way to having a memorable and enjoyable stay in Brazil. Embrace the local culture, meet new people, and make the most of your time in this beautiful country. Have a good trip!
