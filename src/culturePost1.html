Discover the Beauty of Ibirapuera Park in São Paulo

Ibirapuera Park, São Paulo's green oasis, awaits you. Whether you're looking to relax or explore, this urban paradise has it all.

About the park

Ibirapuera Park, located in São Paulo, Brazil, is a sprawling urban oasis beloved by locals and tourists alike. Spanning over 400 acres, this green space offers a retreat from the bustling city life, providing a range of recreational activities and cultural experiences.

History and Development

Opened in 1954 to commemorate the city's 400th anniversary, Ibirapuera Park was designed by renowned Brazilian architect Oscar Niemeyer and landscape designer Roberto Burle Marx. Its name, derived from the indigenous Tupi language, means "rotten tree," reflecting the area's historical use as a marshy region.

Features and Attractions

The park boasts a diverse array of attractions. Visitors can explore its scenic walking trails, rent bicycles, or enjoy a leisurely boat ride on the park's tranquil lakes. Sports enthusiasts can make use of the numerous sports facilities, including soccer fields, tennis courts, and basketball courts. For those interested in culture and history, the park houses several museums and cultural institutions, such as the São Paulo Museum of Modern Art (MAM), the Afro Brazil Museum, and the Ibirapuera Auditorium, known for its distinctive architectural design.

Events and Festivities

Throughout the year, Ibirapuera Park hosts a variety of events and festivities that attract both locals and tourists. These include outdoor concerts, art exhibitions, and traditional Brazilian festivals. The park's vast open spaces provide an ideal venue for large-scale events, making it a hub of cultural and social activities in São Paulo.

Preservation and Conservation

Efforts to preserve and maintain the park's natural beauty and biodiversity are ongoing. The authorities work to ensure that Ibirapuera remains a sustainable and ecologically balanced environment amidst the urban sprawl of São Paulo.

Visiting Ibirapuera Park

Address: Parque Ibirapuera, Av. Pedro Álvares Cabral, s/n - Vila Mariana, São Paulo - SP, 04094-050, Brazil

Opening Hours: The park is open daily from early morning until evening. Specific attractions within the park may have varying operating hours.

Admission: Entry to Ibirapuera Park is generally free, although some attractions and events may have admission fees.

Facilities: Amenities within the park include restrooms, cafes, and information centers where visitors can obtain maps and guidance.
