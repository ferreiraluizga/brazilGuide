Explore the Artistic Wonders of MASP in São Paulo

The São Paulo Museum of Art (MASP) is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring the vibrant city of São Paulo.

About the Museum

The São Paulo Museum of Art (MASP) is one of the most important cultural institutions in Brazil, renowned for its extensive collection of art and its distinctive architectural design. Located on Avenida Paulista in São Paulo, MASP attracts visitors from around the world who come to appreciate its diverse collection and innovative exhibitions..

History and Development

MASP was founded in 1947 by Assis Chateaubriand and Pietro Maria Bardi, with the mission to promote art and culture in São Paulo.

The current building, designed by Italian-Brazilian architect Lina Bo Bardi, was inaugurated in 1968. It is characterized by its iconic red concrete and glass structure, which appears to float above ground level on huge pillars. This design allows the museum's ground floor to remain open, creating a sense of continuity between the museum and the bustling Avenida Paulista below.

Art Collection

    MASP houses a vast collection of over 8,000 artworks, spanning from antiquity to the present day. The collection includes European, Brazilian, African, and Asian art, with significant holdings of paintings, sculptures, photographs, and decorative arts.

    Notable works in the collection include paintings by artists such as Van Gogh, Renoir, Rembrandt, Botticelli, and Portinari, among others. MASP is particularly renowned for its collection of European art, showcasing works from various periods and styles.

Exhibitions and Programs

    MASP hosts dynamic exhibitions that showcase different aspects of its collection, as well as temporary exhibitions featuring contemporary artists from Brazil and around the world.

    The museum offers a range of educational activities, including guided tours, workshops, lectures, and film screenings, aimed at engaging visitors of all ages with art and culture.

Theatrum Mundi:

    One of MASP's distinctive features is its display method known as "Theatrum Mundi." Artworks are presented on clear acrylic easels, allowing visitors to view them in natural light and from various angles. This innovative approach enhances the viewing experience and encourages dialogue about the artworks.

Visitor Information

    Address: Avenida Paulista, 1578, Bela Vista, São Paulo - SP, Brazil, 01310-200

    Opening Hours: Open from Tuesday to Sunday, with extended hours on Thursdays. It is closed on Mondays.

    Admission: Admission fees vary depending on exhibitions and visitor categories. Discounts are available for students, seniors, and certain groups.

    Accessibility: The museum is wheelchair accessible and easily reached by public transportation. The nearest metro stations are Trianon-Masp (Line 2 - Green) and Brigadeiro (Line 2 - Green), both a short walk away.
