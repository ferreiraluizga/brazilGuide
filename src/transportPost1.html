Still don't know how to use São Paulo's Metro and CPTM? Follow these 5 tips:

Discover the best tips for navigating the São Paulo subway and make the most of your trip around the city.

About subway and trains

The subway is a well-known means of transportation worldwide, and in Brazil it is no different. Constantly used by the population, it is one of the main forms of land transportation in the country. We can highlight São Paulo's subway and train system, which resembles in many aspects the subway system of New York, connecting the east, west, north, and south zones of the capital, as well as cities in the interior that have tourist attractions.

Companies Responsible for Operation

Metrô: The São Paulo Metro Company (Metrô) is responsible for operating the subway lines in the city. It manages lines 1 (Blue), 2 (Green), 3 (Red), 4 (Yellow), and 5 (Lilac), covering various areas of the city of São Paulo and neighboring cities.

CPTM: The São Paulo Metropolitan Trains Company (CPTM) is responsible for operating suburban trains that connect São Paulo to several cities in the metropolitan region. CPTM manages several lines, including Line 7 (Ruby), 8 (Diamond), 9 (Emerald), 10 (Turquoise), 11 (Coral), 12 (Sapphire), 13 (Jade), as well as the Airport Express.

Helpful Tips for Using the Subway

    Know the Lines and Stations:

    Familiarize yourself with the metro and train lines. In large cities like São Paulo, there are multiple lines that connect central and peripheral areas. For example, the São Paulo metro includes lines 1-Blue, 2-Green, 3-Red, 4-Yellow, and 5-Lilac, while CPTM trains connect the city to nearby municipalities. Knowing the lines and stations helps you plan your trips efficiently.

    Integration and Tickets:

    Both systems offer integration options between metro lines, trains, and buses, making it easier to travel within the city. Use the Bilhete Único or similar tickets to save on fares for multiple trips. Check for temporal and monthly ticket options if you use public transport frequently. Integration allows seamless transfers between modes without extra cost.

    Schedules and Frequency:

    Metro and train operating hours vary, so check each line’s schedule to plan your trips. Generally, they run from 4:40 AM to midnight, with some variations. Buses also have specific schedules, often synchronized with metro and train times for easy integration.

    Safety and Comfort:

    The metro and trains are generally safe, but always stay alert to your belongings and surroundings. Use designated spaces for passengers with special needs, like the elderly and pregnant women, and take advantage of carriages for cyclists when appropriate. Follow conduct rules and safety regulations for a smooth journey.

    Bonus Tip:

    Use official metro and train apps for detailed schedule information, route planning, and service alerts. These apps provide real-time updates, interactive maps, bus schedules, and route options, helping you plan your trips efficiently.

With these tips, you're well-equipped to navigate the city's public transport system. Enjoy visiting São Paulo's top tourist attractions efficiently and safely. Have a great trip!
